These methods involve the decomposition of organic materials, such as food waste, yard trimmings, and agricultural by-products, by microorganisms under controlled conditions. Composting, a process that occurs in the presence of oxygen, results in the production of nutrient-rich compost that can be used as a soil amendment in agriculture and landscaping. Anaerobic digestion, which occurs in the absence of oxygen, produces biogas, primarily composed of methane, which can be used as a renewable energy source. These processes are environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional waste disposal methods, as they help reduce the volume of waste sent to landfills, minimize greenhouse gas emissions, and create valuable by-products that can be used in agriculture or energy production.
